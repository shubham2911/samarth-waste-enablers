Samarth Waste-Enablers

We at Samarth Waste-Enablers are formalizing the Waste Management Industry, empowering our local heroes, THE WASTE PICKERS by introducing a
fintech intervention to bring systemic change in the society.

Lack of Financial Security

- 3 million Waste Pickers in India struggle to make ends meet.
- Barely earning daily wages based on the type of waste they encounter.

Health and Safety Issues

- Waste Pickers are informally employed with no protective gear as well as no Health Insurance.
- Life Expectancy of a Waste Picker is 39 years of age (versus 69 years for an average Indian) because of the dangerous working conditions (fumes, fires, dirt).

Untapped Potential of Waste

- There is untapped value in waste. 81% of waste in India is untreated (50 million tonnes every year), and half of total waste (62 million tonnes total waste every year) is dumped in landfill.
- Regularization in informal Sector can lead to more Recycling of Waste.

Lack of Recognition and Dignity

- Collection by Waste Pickers is the only mechanism to collect recyclables as of now. The informal recycling sector contributes to 95% of all recycling happening in India.
- Waste Pickers hail from the most margalised communities in urban spaces and their marginalization contributes to lack of recognition of their work to the society.
